Kawchu/Rubber Tree

installation, 2022

The colloquial name "rubber tree" isn't just a meaningless name, but a long-forgotten ghost of the colonial history of rubber extraction. Ficus elastica (Gummibaum) has succeeded in becoming a very common and popular household plant and is the "face" for the name "rubber tree". While Hevea brasiliensis (Kautschukbaum), the species most used in the extraction of natural rubber, is distant from our lives, evokes no images, and is disassociated from a living form, turned into just raw material.

This installation was a space to share my research, discoveries, and artistic experiments on the history of the natural rubber industry.

Ñ Sou Fruta

stickers and instagram filter, 2019

Ñ Sou Fruta (I’m not a fruit) is a project about the use of the term "exotic" applied to people, imposing an otherness and foreignness to a person. The idea was inspired by a brand and the stickers on the packaging of their tropical fruits. This playful project personates how the term is used for both fruit and people, often fetishizing the tropics and colonized countries, cultures, and bodies.

For this project, 10 stickers were developed, for 10 different tropical fruits, each of them with rhymes referencing the food brand’s style. This design was then applied in two forms: a small photo series and an Instagram filter.*

*Unfortunately, the filter is no longer available since an update on the Instagram platform.

Bá ne'ebé? Percursos Visuais em Timor-Leste

editorial design, 2019

”A fotografia como artefato político” (photography as a political artifact).

Bá ne'ebé? Percursos Visuais em Timor-Leste is a photography book that captured the experience and work done between the Anthropology department of the University of Brasilia and the National University of East Timor.

A collaborative editorial project between Sarah Alvim and Pedro Joffily.

GIFLife

interactive installation + video collage, 2018/2019

The artist performs a series of GIFs in a scene-loop for a predetermined time. GIFs represent what you feel and act, you act and feel what they represent. The virtually mundane becomes itself an artistic production and subject.

GIFLife was an interactive installation, later on, adapted into a video collage. The installation functions as a clickbait to take photos and collect comments from viewers. Video performances of gifs were set to play one after the other in an app interface made with Max MSP. Whenever someone liked, disliked, or commented on the app GIFLife, a photo was taken and saved together with the input. The images captured were printed and pinned to the wall by the side of the installation.

The subsequent video-collage piece has a duration of 1 hour with each recorded gif-performance being 15 minutes.

(I'm)The Mountain

video performance, 2019

Inspired by the existential game The Mountain by David O'Reilly, an appropriation of the gamer youtube culture is made by a game-play video of The Mountain. The end result is a 3 hours video of continuous game-play with a live video-audio recording with textual comments.

Instead of narrating the game as it is usually done, I mimic the Mountain (game “character”) and stand by contemplative, with a text window where I could share my personal thoughts throughout the day. To watch this performance video is to accompany the existential Mountain living its life and sharing some of its thoughts, while I, as the player, take the same position.
